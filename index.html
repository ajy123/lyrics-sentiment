The Emotions of Pop Songs

In their 2018 research, "Quantitative Sentiment Analysis of Lyrics in Popular Music", tresearchers described how the lyrics of popular music have trended towards a more negative tone, characterized by emotions such as anger, disgust, fear, sadness, and conscientiousness. This analysis covered Billboard 100 songs from 1951 to 2016. Does this trend continue in recent years?

By examining Spotify's weekly Top 200 playlists from 2017 to 2021, we can explore this question further. The chart on the right illustrates the distribution of songs across different emotional categories (positive, slightly positive, slightly negative, negative) for each week.
Each dot is a song in Top200 weekly playlist.

Most pop songs' lyrics have fluctuated between slightly positive and slightly negative emotions over this period.

However, a notable trend emerged in early 2020: listeners seemed to prefer songs with more extreme emotional content, likely reflecting the uncertainty and anxiety of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Methodology and Notes
Emotional categories are determined by spaCy's sentiment analysis, which assigns a sentiment score between -1 and 1 to each song. These scores are categorized as follows:

  • Positive: scores between 0.5 and 1
  • Slightly Positive: scores between 0 and 0.5
  • Slightly Negative: scores between -0.5 and 0
  • Negative: scores between -1 and -0.5
